WHAT TO DO

SIGHTS

  • MUSEUMS:

    Royal Museum of Fine Arts (Beaux-Arts), Magritte Museum , Boghossian Foundation - Villa Empain , Musical Instruments Museum , Comics Art Museum.

  • PARKS:

    Bois de la Cambre , Brussels Park , Cinquantenaire.

  • LANDMARKS:

    Grand-Place , Atomium , Manneken Pis (overrated, it's just a fountain with a statue of a boy peeing... somehow there are also statues of a girl peeing and a dog peeing ), National Basilica of the Sacred Heart.

  • EUROPEAN QUARTER:

    European Parliament , Council , Berlaymont (main EU Commission building).

FOOD

  • OUR GO-TOS:

    Nona Pasta (their pizza place next door is also amazing), Rambo (smash burgers, also has vegetarian alternative), Little Apo (Vietnamese), Ma Jolie.

  • OTHER FAVOURITES:

    Les Brasseries Georges (traditional French), My Tannour (Syrian), Palo Alto (Italian-Catalan), Carbon (Chinese), Takumi (Ramen).

  • BELGIAN:

    Fernand-obb , Maison Antoine (for more traditional fries), Friterie Patatak (best fries in our opinion), Maison Dandoy (waffles).

  • VEGAN*:

    Liu Lin (Taiwanese), Mo Mo (Tibetan dumplings), Oui Oui (ice-cream).

  • BRUNCH:

    Chicago Café , Frank (for evenings, the place transforms into Francine , a vegan/vegetarian forward restaurant).

  • GOOD ALSO FOR APÉRO/SMALL BITES OR JUST DRINKS:

    Chez Richard , Chez Franz (also brunch on Sundays).

DRINKS

    Vertigo (best cocktails), L'Archiduc (art déco bar opened since 1937), Marcelle (Gin & tonics), Cobra (whiskeys and whiskies), Edgar's Flavors , Brussels Beer Project (Dansaert/Bailli), Café Belga , L'Athénée , Delirium Café (more than 2000 types of beer, overrated in our opinion).

*Aside from these three, most others on the list have vegan options, so we recommend checking their menus if you are interested).
